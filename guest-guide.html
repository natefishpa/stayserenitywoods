Guest Guide

A peaceful rural stay works best when everyone knows the basics.

This page keeps the important parts public and simple. Final check-in details, codes, and any sensitive instructions should stay inside Airbnb/VRBO messages, not on the open internet.

The short version

Enjoy the property. Respect the land. Keep the peace.

Serenity Woods is a rural, nature-forward property. That means space, quiet, wildlife, trees, a creek, a pool, and the normal realities of the country. Guests should supervise children, stay within property boundaries, follow pool/fire safety, and move late-night gathering indoors after quiet hours.

Property boundaries

Stay on the Serenity Woods property and do not enter neighboring land. Rural property lines and posted signs matter.

Quiet hours

Outdoor noise should shut down by 11 PM. Sound carries through the valley at night, so late-night gatherings should move inside.

Pool safety

The pool is seasonal and should be used responsibly. Children need close supervision, no glass belongs in the pool area, and guests should follow posted or booking-platform rules.

Creek and woods

The creek and wooded areas are beautiful but natural. Expect uneven terrain, slippery rocks, ticks, wildlife, slopes, and changing conditions after rain.

Fire pit

Use the fire pit responsibly and skip fires during dry-weather advisories. Put fires fully out before leaving the area or going to bed.

Connectivity

The house has fast fiber internet and Wi‑Fi, which helps because cell reception can be spotty in rural areas.

Best fit for guests

Great for families with confident swimmers, adults wanting a quiet friend retreat, couples exploring the Finger Lakes, and groups who want nature without being too far from small-town supplies.

Keep sensitive details private

Door codes, Wi‑Fi passwords, camera/monitoring details, and operational instructions should stay in the booking platform messages or a private guest document.

Questions before booking?

Use the listing platforms for current rules, availability, rates, and messaging with the host.
